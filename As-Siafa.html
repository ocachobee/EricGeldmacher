    As-Siafa Vegetation

    Back to other Disaters

    As As-Siafa and surrounding areas were bombarded by military armaments in 2023-2025. As a result the surrounding vegetation has taken a toll.

    _____________________

    This false color composite shows vegetation in red using infrared wavelengths.


    _____________________

    This image taken in September of 2023 before the bombing started shows a field in As-Siafa.



    According to the NDVI test of the above area, this field was healthy. For these NDVI representations green represents health in plants as NDVI measures chlorophyll in plants.



    A year later we see much fewer green pixels in this field, showing us that these fields plants are not as healthy.



    Two years after the original NDVI assessment we can see that this field has no living plants due to the lack of green.

    _____________________

    Gaza Strip NDVI and Vegetation Classification

    The above images displayed a single field in the Gaza strip area, but let’s take a look at it on a larger scale and quantify it. Using the same NDVI images we can then classify the data using unsupervised classification into two separate groups. These groups naturally become vegetation and no vegetation. However, doing this I encountered a problem, the vegetation in 2025 and 2024 are different in the greater Israel area not just the Gaza strip. This could be due to a number of reasons, such as less rainfall, but in order to take this into account I preformed unsupervised classification on a larger area that had lots of vegetation outside of the Gaza Strip, then masked the results to just the Gaza Strip. This provides us with information more relative to the year and less relative to separate years.






    Looking at this chart below you can see before the bombing started the Gaza strip had 18.31 percent of its surface area as healthy plants, dropping to 3.05 and 5.30 percent in 2024 and 2025 respectively. This clearly demonstrates the conflicts effect on the vegetation reducing it by over 600% in 2024.
